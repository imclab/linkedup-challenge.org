Veni Competition


Abstracts from entries

The Veni Competiton ran between late May and late June 2013. People were invited to design and build innovative and robust prototypes and demos for tools that analyse and/or integrate open web data for educational purposes.


We received 22 valid entries. The abstracts are given below. All entries are now being reviewed by the evaluation committee, led by the LinkedUp advisory board. The advisory board will create a shortlist by 16 August 2013. Those shortlisted will be invited to OKCon in Geneva. The LinkedUp Award Ceremony will take place at OKCon on Tuesday 17 September, where first, second, and third prizes will be awarded. There will also be a prize awarded for the ‘people’s choice’.



Veni Competition


Deadline: 27 June 2013

We’re inviting you to design and build innovative and robust prototypes and demos for tools that analyse and/or integrate open web data for educational purposes, and have compiled a catalogue of data sets to get you started.

The idea is to mash up the vast amount of material and data on the internet that can be used in education, ranging from course data, lecture slides, and online courses to Wikipedia articles and YouTube videos.

You might design a tool for combining datasets and visualising them in interesting new ways, or perhaps develop a search engine that combines search results from different libraries. Please see our use cases if you are looking for further inspiration.

Alternatively, you may already have an idea that you’ve started developing and we’re keen to help you take this further.

The prizes:

  • 1st prize - 2000 EUR
  • 2nd + 3rd prize - 1000 EUR
  • 5 runner up prizes worth 200 EUR each.

The second competition will launch in November 2013, and the third in May 2014. Please sign up to our mailing list for further updates.
